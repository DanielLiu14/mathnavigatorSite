Top 10 Habitable Exoplanets

  1. K2-18b
  2. Kepler-22b
  3. Proxima Centauri b
  4. Europa
  5. Kepler-452b
  6. Trappist 1 System
  7. Gliese-667Cc
  8. Ross-128b
  9. Mars
  10. Titan

    11. 1. Located 110 light years from Earth,K2-18b is a recently discovered exoplanet in the habitable zone that has water vapor detected in its atmosphere.

    2. While Kepler-22b is located 587 ligth years from Earth in the constellation Cygnus, this exoplanet orbits a Sun-like star. Further discovery could be made with more powerful telescopes.

    3. Proxima Centauri b is the closest planet to Earth located 4 light years from Earth. It orbits a red dwarf star 20 times closer than the Earth orbiting the sun. It might be able to host alien life.

    4. Europa is one of Jupiter's Gallileian moons that contains oceans of water 10 to 15 miles below the thick icy crust. The ocean stretches up to 40 to 100 miles in depth. The Europa Clipper could launch in 2025 to look for alien life.

    5. Kepler-452b is located 1,110 light years from Earth that has been confirmed to be the first near-Earth planet in the habitable zone

    6. The Trappist 1 System is a system of 7 Earth-like planets discovered in 2017. 3 of the 7 planets are in the habitable zone and could contain liquid water.

    7. Gliese-667Cc is an exoplanet orbiting the star Gliese-667Cc which is a triple star system. The exoplanet has a landscape just like Star Wars Tatoonie and it could host alien life located 23 light years from Earth.

    8. Ross-128b is discovered to be 11 light years from Earth, the second nearest exoplanet to Earth. It orbits a very inactive red dwarf star which could make it possible for alien life to exist

    9. Mars is one of the planets in our solar system that many space companies such as NASA and SpaceX are trying to go to and attempt to colonize the planet. The First space mission to Mars could be in the 2030s but NASA wants to get humans to Mars by 2035.

    10. Have you heard of the moon that is bigger than planet Mercury? Well Titan is that planet. It is one of the best moons because it has methane liquid lakes and oceans on its surface. There is a good chance that alien life exists there. NASA plans to send its Dragonfly Mission to Titan launching in 2026 and arriving in 2034. Dragonfly will fly many miles through multiple areas to examine sites around the moon and examine what chemicals are there.
